No-Churn Ice Cream

The cream cheese base that changed everything

Glen of Glen & Friends Cooking is an Ontario-based YouTuber who makes approachable recipes with a focus on doing things right. In 2019 he published a simple 3-ingredient no-churn ice cream (condensed milk, cream, vanilla). By 2021, he'd evolved the recipe into something better: a 5-ingredient cream cheese base that's tangier, creamier, and stays scoopable for days. That base became the foundation for over a dozen flavor variations on his channel, from Rum & Raisin to Toblerone to Coffee Crisp. This page is a cross-referenced compilation of those recipes.

Why It Works

The science behind five ingredients

Cream Cheese

This is Glen's key move. Most no-churn recipes are just condensed milk and whipped cream, which tastes one-note sweet. Cream cheese adds tang that balances the sugar, creates a richer mouthfeel, and acts as a stabilizer that keeps the texture smooth after days in the freezer. Think of it like adding sour cream to a cake batter. Same principle.

Whipped Cream

An ice cream machine works by incorporating air while freezing. No-churn skips the machine by whipping air into the cream first, then folding everything together. The trapped air bubbles prevent ice crystals from forming and give you that light, scoopable texture. This is why gentle folding matters so much. Stir too hard and you deflate all the air you just whipped in.

Condensed Milk

Traditional ice cream uses an egg custard base cooked on the stovetop. Condensed milk sidesteps all of that. It's already a concentrated emulsion of sugar, milk proteins, and fat. The high sugar content lowers the freezing point, which prevents the ice cream from freezing into a solid block and wards off freezer burn.

Salt

Half a teaspoon seems small but it does real work. It suppresses the perception of sweetness (condensed milk is very sweet), brings out whatever flavor you're adding, and improves the overall mouthfeel. Skip it and the ice cream tastes flat.

The Ice Cream Base

Cream cheese era, 2021+

Servings
6

Method

1
Beat the cream cheese

Beat cream cheese in a large bowl until smooth and fluffy. You want it light and airy before adding anything else.

2
Build the base

Add vanilla, salt, and condensed milk to the cream cheese. Beat until combined and smooth. Set this bowl aside.

3
Whip the cream

In a separate chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream to stiff peaks. Stop the moment you get there. Over-whipping turns cream into butter.

4
Fold together

Gently fold whipped cream into the cream cheese mixture in 2-3 additions. Fold, don't stir. You're preserving the air that will give this ice cream its texture.

5
Add your flavor

If making a flavored version, fold in your add-ins now.

6
Freeze

Pour into a freezer-safe container. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface (this blocks ice crystals). Freeze at least 6 hours. Overnight is better.

Glen's Tips

Cold cream matters. Keep it in the fridge until the moment you whip it. A chilled metal bowl helps too.

Room temp cream cheese. If it's cold you'll get lumps. Let it sit out 30-45 minutes.

Plastic wrap on the surface. This is the single best thing you can do to prevent ice crystals.

If it's too hard to scoop, let it sit on the counter for 5-10 minutes. Don't microwave it.

Glen's "1 can" = 300 mL (Canadian). In the US that's a 14 oz can. Close enough.
